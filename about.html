About Me

I’m a Computer Engineering student at the Universitat Politècnica de València (UPV) with a strong focus on quantitative finance, data science, and software engineering.

My work revolves around applying data-driven analytical and programming skills to real-world financial challenges — particularly in algorithmic trading, risk modelling, and machine learning.

I recently completed an internship at Ernst & Young (EY) within the Tax & Quantitative Services (TQS) division, where I contributed to the preparation of technical documentation for innovation projects, performed analytical cost modelling, and supported financial validation processes for clients under Spanish R&D tax frameworks.

I’m also an active member of the Software Group at the UPV Investment Club, where we develop and backtest algorithmic trading strategies, research quantitative techniques for portfolio optimization, and participate in student-led finance competitions.

I’m passionate about bridging technology, data, and financial markets, continuously learning and building a foundation for a future career in quantitative research and financial engineering.
